Process Journal

Introduction

Our design project is an Autonomous high-rise window washer it will provide a low cost and efficient solution for cleaning the high-rise glass surface to instead of manual cleaning. In this process journal I will go through the process of designing this project.

Week 1 and week 2

To start with our design project we need to choose a topic which we are going to work for the next 4 months. It has to be something which interests us, something autonomic and something which is going to solve some problems in our life. The first thing that came to my mind was to make a brainstorm of ideas:

Figure 1.1 - Brainstorm topic

For the brainstorm, the topic we find the most interesting, the one we think it could help to make a change and it can help some people in their life is Autonomic high-rise window washer: This has been a project that has called my attention a few days earlier. Just a few days ago, our apartment building was doing window cleaning. When I saw the window washer hanging themselves on the outside of the building to clean the windows of each unit, I was unconsciously worried about them. Even though I knew they were professional and they had enough protection they need.

    Previous Next
    Problem: What is the problem being tackled?

    To start with this stage, I’ve been researching a lot about the market review for high-rise window washer worldwide. I found that the cost of high-rise window washer is increasing every year from 2010 to 2018. Moreover, according to the International Window Cleaning Association, there had been one high-rise fatality per year in the last four years in the U.S., that’s with an estimated 1.5 million “exposures”-when workers are on the sides of buildings—a year.So based on the current investigation, the problems that need to be solved are the high cost of the high-rise window cleaning and the Safety issues when working at high altitude.

    Target Audience (Who is the research sponsor? Who to keep involved?)

    I decided to identify the target audience through user surveys and online research. Through previous surveys, high-rise window washers are one of my target audiences. The questions I made in the questionnaire mainly focused on whether the participants needed to clean the windows themselves, the time it takes and is it difficult to clean the windows by themselves. By collecting the answers to the questionnaire, I sorted out two groups of people. People living in the Town House needed to clean the glass of the house themselves and the people living in the apartment because they had a building manager so they did not need to clean the glass themselves.

    Previous Next

    After the collected the data from questionnaires, I divided the people who participated in the survey into two categories (people living in the townhouse need to clean their windows by themselves and people live in the apartment, they usually have building managers, and the building manager will be responsible for the window cleaning). To sum up, my target audience is divided into three categories, people living in Town House, building managers and high-rise window washers.So far I decided that I will interview people from two different professions, building managers and high-rise window washers. I listed some questions that I need to ask during the interview. My interview with the building manager focused on the time required to complete a window cleaning of the building, the amount of money required to complete a window cleaning of the building, the requirements of the high-rise window washers when the building manager hired the cleaning company, and the preparations required before the building was cleaned etc.

    For high-rise window washers my questions will mainly focus on the safety and time: the risk factors encountered in cleaning the building window, whether the weather will affect the work, the time required to clean a building, as a high-rise window washer what is the most difficult things you encounter when you work? After interviewing with building managers, I found that they had to pay the cleaning company for up to $10,000 each time, and they needed a window cleaning in about 3 months.

    Figure 4.1 - High-rise window washer doing washering

    Saving time and saving money become two of the problems we need to solve. After interviewing with some high-rise window washer, I found that they need to do a lot of safety check before work. The weather will affect the efficiency of work sometimes the change of weather will bring danger to work, finally cleaning takes a long time. So safety issues are a concern for them and saving time also is a problem we need to solve.

    Existing products:

    I also did some background checks on drones. Most of the clean drones on the market today can only be cleaned at a certain height and need to be manually operated.

    Base on the feedback I received on my task 1, I have brainstorm ideas for addressing the identified context, user requirements and develop two ideas into design concepts using storyboard. To start with this stage, Our group discussed two different concepts, one for the business and the other for the individual user.The concept of the enterprise is also divided into two parts, basic and professional.

    The basic interface is mainly focused on automatic, and the main target is the building manager. The target audience of the professional interface is the high-rise window cleaning, which is used to handle special situations, for example, the special shape of the building and the windy day which automatic drone cannot handle the situation, requiring professionals to manually operate and clean.     low-fidelity prototypes

    After studying the tutorial in week 6 ‘making and evaluation low-fidelity prototypes’. We started to make rough hand-drawn sketches based on the users, used as interface drawings for design prototypes. In the beginning, we made a hand-sketch interface and used it for a user evaluation. During the evaluation, we found some problems such as:

    Figure 2.2 - Low fidelity prototypes

  • Don’t have a page of connecting
  • The differences between basic and pro are not clearly
  • Too complex and difficult to understand if there are no annotates
  • The sign-up page is not clear.
  • Not very fluency.
    • We made a page iteration based on these comments and made a low-fidelity prototype. We took the user's opinion and made another iteration and applied it to the wireframe afterwards.

    Wireframe

    After conducting the survey for the development of this application. We found that both concepts have a certain amount of market demand. By arranging feedback from the evaluation，most of the participants believe that the two concepts can be combined and improved, so that the user can could adopt the application into their business and lifestyle.

After making the mockups.
